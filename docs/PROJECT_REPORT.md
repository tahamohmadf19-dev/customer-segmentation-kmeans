# Project Report: Customer Segmentation Using K-Means Clustering

## Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of customer segmentation using K-Means clustering on the Online Retail II dataset. The project successfully identified 3-4 distinct customer groups based on RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) metrics, enabling targeted marketing strategies and improved customer retention.

                                     ## 3. Methodology

                                     ### 3.1 Data Preprocessing

                                     **Step 1: Data Cleaning**
                                     - Remove records with missing CustomerID (essential for segmentation)
                                     - - Filter negative quantities (cancelled orders)
                                       - - Handle duplicates and inconsistencies
                                         - - Convert data types appropriately
                                          
                                           - **Step 2: Feature Engineering**
                                           - - Calculate transaction metrics per customer:
                                             -   - **Recency (R)**: Days since last purchase
                                                 -   - **Frequency (F)**: Total number of transactions
                                                     -   - **Monetary (M)**: Total spending amount
                                                         - - Create additional features as needed
                                                          
                                                           - **Step 3: Outlier Detection**
                                                           - - Use statistical methods (IQR) to identify extreme values
                                                             - - Decide on treatment (removal vs. capping)
                                                               - - Validate business implications of outlier handling
                                                                
                                                                 - ### 3.2 RFM Analysis
                                                                
                                                                 - **Recency**: Days between last purchase and analysis date
                                                                 - - Formula: max_date - last_purchase_date
                                                                   - - Interpretation: Lower is better (recent engagement)
                                                                    
                                                                     - **Frequency**: Number of distinct transactions per customer
                                                                     - - Formula: COUNT(transactions) per customer
                                                                       - - Interpretation: Higher indicates loyal customers
                                                                        
                                                                         - **Monetary**: Total revenue generated per customer
                                                                         - - Formula: SUM(Quantity * UnitPrice) per customer
                                                                           - - Interpretation: Higher indicates valuable customers
                                                                            
                                                                             - **Normalization**: Standardize R, F, M using StandardScaler
                                                                             - - Zero mean and unit variance
                                                                               - - Essential for K-Means algorithm
                                                                                
                                                                                 - ### 3.3 K-Means Clustering
                                                                                
                                                                                 - **Algorithm Overview**:
                                                                                 - K-Means partitions customers into k clusters by minimizing within-cluster variance.
                                                                                
                                                                                 - **Implementation Steps**:
                                                                                 - 1. **Determine Optimal k**:
                                                                                   2.    - Elbow Method: Plot inertia vs. k
                                                                                         -    - Analyze where WCSS (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares) decreases significantly
                                                                                              -    - Typical range: k = 2 to 5
                                                                                               
                                                                                                   - 2. **Fit Model**:
                                                                                                     3.    - Initialize centroids randomly or using k-means++
                                                                                                           -    - Iteratively assign points to nearest centroid
                                                                                                                -    - Update centroids based on cluster means
                                                                                                                     -    - Repeat until convergence (centroid movement < threshold)
                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                          - 3. **Assign Clusters**:
                                                                                                                            4.    - Classify each customer to nearest cluster centroid
                                                                                                                                  -    - Store cluster labels for interpretation
                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                       - ### 3.4 Evaluation Metrics
                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                       - #### Elbow Method
                                                                                                                                       - - **Metric**: Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS)
                                                                                                                                         - - **Calculation**: Sum of squared distances from each point to its cluster centroid
                                                                                                                                           - - **Interpretation**: Lower WCSS = tighter clusters
                                                                                                                                             - - **Decision**: Find "elbow" point where improvement diminishes
                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                               - #### Silhouette Score
                                                                                                                                               - - **Range**: -1 to 1
                                                                                                                                                 - - **Interpretation**:
                                                                                                                                                   -   - Score > 0.5: Strong cluster structure
                                                                                                                                                       -   - Score 0.4-0.5: Reasonable separation
                                                                                                                                                           -   - Score < 0.3: Weak clustering
                                                                                                                                                               - - **Formula**: (b - a) / max(a, b) per sample
                                                                                                                                                                 -   - a = mean distance to points in same cluster
                                                                                                                                                                     -   - b = mean distance to points in nearest cluster
                                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                         - #### Visual Inspection
                                                                                                                                                                         - - Scatter plots of R, F, M by cluster
                                                                                                                                                                           - - Cluster size distribution
                                                                                                                                                                             - - Centroid characteristics
